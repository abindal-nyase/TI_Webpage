## Audiences

Three primary audiences. Hired by architect, not owner (typically).

| Audience | Top Criteria |
|----------|-------------|
| Architect | Professionalism, responsiveness, fast turnaround. Not creativity/innovation. |
| Building Owner | Existing relationship (P1), familiarity with building (P2), cost (P3). |
| Property/Project Manager | Familiarity with company, competence, proven experience. High personal risk if engineer underperforms. |

**Who initiates TI work:** Owners 40% · Architects 40% · Project Managers 20%

**Recommendation ecosystem:** Building managers → architects. Architects are the hub. Perceived as "same tribe" — reliable, clear, responsible.

## Messaging

- **Self-description:** Partners, Advisory and Service (not just engineers)

- **Core statement:** WE'RE NOT JUST ENGINEERS. WE'RE THE TEAM THAT GETS IT DONE.

### Four Pillars

1. **We don't stall.** Pick up the phone, clear roadblocks, move. TI work isn't linear — we thrive in decisions, deadlines, drawings.
2. **We speak architect.** Feel like part of their office. Design partners, not consultants.
3. **We build trust.** Project managers bring us in to protect their reputation.
4. **We reduce friction.** Know your buildings, your systems, how to avoid rework.

### Audience-Specific Closing Copy

- *Architect:* juggling demanding client + stacked schedule → engineers on speed dial
- *Owner:* retaining tenants with minimal disruption → already know your structure
- *PM:* zero margin for error → safe hands your team will thank you for

## What We Do Best (4 Service Areas)

### High-Rise TI Projects
Fast, detail-perfect work in occupied towers. Complex stair design, load transfer in constrained grids, fast feasibility for in-field questions.

### Lobby Renovations
Relationship-driven, creativity required. Visible, high-stakes, structural surprises. Seamless structure for creative rebrands, new entries/stairs/ceilings, deep knowledge of aging high-rise frames.

### Fast-Turn TI Fit-Outs
Built for velocity. Lightning-fast partition/ceiling support, lean drawings that answer field questions, flexibility for multi-floor rollout.

### The Invisible Details
Devil is in the connections. Anchors, edge-of-slab issues, hidden supports. Retrofit strategies that avoid triggering major upgrades. Coordination with architectural nuance.

**Triggers for SE onboarding:** stairs and partitions.

## Strategic Notes

- High-rises should be primary focus (anyone can do 2-story retail TI)
- Lobby work: building owners hire for this; relationship-driven; requires creativity
- Small architects: position as good partners/collaborators — guidance, not superiority
- High-level architects: demonstrate ability to follow along for high-end clients
- Peer review offering available for owners
- Decisions sometimes made by committee (interior architects + interior designers)
